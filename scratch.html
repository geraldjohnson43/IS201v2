10 Facts About Chimps

  1. Chimpanzees are one of the closest living relatives of humans, sharing more than 98 percent of our genetic blueprint.
  2. Chimps are intelligent and social animals, living in groups of up to 150 individuals.
  3. Chimps use tools, such as sticks to extract insects from trees or rocks to crack open nuts.
  4. Chimps have a diverse diet, consisting of fruits, leaves, insects, and occasionally small mammals.
    • Fruits, such as figs, papayas, and bananas
    • Leaves, such as young leaves, flowers, and buds
    • Insects, such as termites, ants, and caterpillars
    • Small mammals, such as monkeys and bushbabies (less commonly eaten)
    • Eggs, such as bird eggs and reptile eggs (less commonly eaten)
  5. Chimps are capable of recognizing themselves in mirrors, which is a sign of self-awareness.
  6. Chimps communicate with each other using a variety of vocalizations, gestures, and facial expressions.
  7. Chimps have a lifespan of around 40 to 50 years in the wild.
  8. Chimps are threatened by habitat loss, hunting, and disease, and are classified as endangered by the IUCN.
  9. Chimps have been the subject of scientific research for decades, contributing to our understanding of genetics, behavior, and evolution.
  10. Chimps are also featured in popular culture, appearing in movies, TV shows, and books.
